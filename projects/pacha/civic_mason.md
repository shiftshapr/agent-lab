CIVIC MASON — PG FILM / SERIES PREMISE (PACHA AGE 18)

CORE CONCEPT
**Civic Mason** is the young adult evolution of Pacha — now 18 — stepping into the real world as a **builder of systems, communities, and culture**.

Where Pacha once explored dream worlds, Civic Mason navigates the **real-world equivalents**:
food systems, economies, governance, and identity — all now tangible, complex, and at stake.

---

PREMISE
At 18, Pacha begins to realize that the dream worlds she once visited — Foodlandia, SATS, and beyond — were not just dreams.

They were **blueprints**.

Now, in a world facing real crises — broken food systems, extractive economies, fragmented communities — she steps into a new role:

> Not just a dreamer… but a **builder**.

She takes on the identity of **Civic Mason** — one who helps **design and rebuild the systems that shape society**.

---

TONE & FORMAT

* PG (accessible, inspiring, grounded but elevated)
* Genre: Coming-of-age + social adventure + light sci-fi realism
* Tone: Hopeful, intelligent, emotionally grounded, culturally rich
* Format:

  * Feature film OR
  * Serialized (8–10 episode seasons)

---

WORLD
Set in a near-present, slightly elevated reality where:

* Technology, community, and culture are deeply intertwined
* Systems are visible and discussable (food, money, governance)
* Young people are beginning to question and reshape the world

Subtle remnants of the dreamworld remain:

* Visual echoes
* Symbolic parallels
* Occasional intuitive “guidance” moments

---

MAIN CHARACTER

PACHA (18) / CIVIC MASON

* Thoughtful, creative, observant
* Carries deep empathy and quiet leadership
* Struggles with stepping into responsibility
* Learns to move from **awareness → action → leadership**

Her arc:

> From “I see what’s wrong” → “I can help fix it” → “We can build something new together”

---

SUPPORTING CHARACTERS

CORE CREW (MODERN REFLECTIONS OF DREAM ALLIES)

* **The Grower** (Foodlandia parallel)
  Urban farmer / food activist — grounded, practical

* **The Coder** (SATS parallel)
  Builder of decentralized tools — systems thinker

* **The Organizer** (SATS II parallel)
  Community mobilizer — governance and coordination

* **The Artist** (SATS III parallel)
  Identity, culture, and narrative shaper

Together, with Compute, they form a **real-world “node” of change**.

---

ANTAGONISTIC FORCES

Not a single villain — but systems:

* Extractive corporations
* Attention economies
* Broken governance structures
* Cultural fragmentation

Optional embodied antagonist:
**A charismatic systems architect** who believes control and efficiency matter more than participation and humanity.

---

STORY ENGINE (SERIES POTENTIAL)

Each arc mirrors earlier dream layers:

SEASON / ACT 1 — FOOD (FOODLANDIA)

* Food deserts, supply chains, cultural food loss
* Civic Mason helps launch a community food initiative

SEASON / ACT 2 — VALUE (SATS I)

* Economic inequality, creator exploitation
* Builds alternative value systems / platforms

SEASON / ACT 3 — GOVERNANCE (SATS II)

* Group decision-making, power struggles
* Experiments with new governance models

SEASON / ACT 4 — IDENTITY (SATS III)

* Culture, belonging, digital identity
* Shapes narrative and collective identity
