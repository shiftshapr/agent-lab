# Rebranding the Metaweb to Overweb

## Submitter

**Eric Schneider**
eric@jpberlin.de

## Overview

This submission addresses a significant brand perception issue: the public confusion between the Metaweb and the Metaverse. The Metaverse, associated with corporate overreach and dystopian imagery, negatively colors first impressions of the Metaweb. This proposal suggests rebranding to a term like 'Overweb'—a name that feels assertive, memorable, and true to the vision of an interoperable, human-centered web infrastructure. This submission was generated with protocol META-DP-EVAL-v1.3.

### DP19 - Amplifying Presence and Community Engagement

### DP20 - Community Ownership

Advocates for a name that reflects grassroots identity and shared authorship rather than co-opted corporate branding.

## Clarifications and Extensions

### DP19 - Amplifying Presence and Community Engagement — Clarification

**Overweb as Memetic Anchor**

The term 'Overweb' offers intuitive resonance, framing the Meta-Layer as something above and beyond traditional web systems, and sidestepping metaphoric confusion with dystopian 'Metaverse' narratives.

*Why it matters:* Names shape public discourse. A clear, distinct, and resonant name helps communities rally, media to clarify, and the public to differentiate between extractive and empowering digital futures.
