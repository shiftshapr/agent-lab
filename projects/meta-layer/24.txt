## Overview

Based on Dr. Paul John Werbos's Saving the World and New Jerusalem posts, this submission proposes clarifications and extensions to numerous Desirable Properties to account for the governance, authentication, and accountability of advanced non-human agents and AI systems, including those that may operate in physical or hybrid embodiments, participate in economic systems, or manage critical infrastructure. This submission was generated with protocol META-DP-EVAL-v1.3.

## Clarifications and Extensions

### DP1 – Federated Authentication & Accountability — Clarification

**Extending Accountability to Non-Human Agents**

Clarify how advanced AI systems and embodied agents are authenticated, how their actions are tracked, and how accountability is enforced.

*Why it matters:* Federated accountability must encompass AGI and complex systems managing critical infrastructure.

### DP2 – Participant Agency and Empowerment — Clarification

**Defining Agency for AI and Systems**

Define what 'agency' means for non-human agents, especially those with action vectors and AI autonomy.

*Why it matters:* To ensure the Meta-layer accommodates AGI entities as coherent participants with defined roles.

### DP3 – Adaptive Governance Supporting an Exponentially Growing Community — Extension

**Including System Complexity in Governance**

Governance must adapt to include advanced AI systems as contributors, regulators, or infrastructure operators.

*Why it matters:* The scope of governance should extend beyond population growth to complexity of agents.

### DP6 – Commerce — Clarification

**Non-Human Participation in Value Exchange**

Clarify whether non-human agents can own, generate, or transact value within the Meta-layer.

*Why it matters:* Ensuring the commerce layer includes mechanisms for AI economic interactions.
