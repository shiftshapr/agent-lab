## Submitter

**Eric Schneider**
eric@jpberlin.de

## Overview

Early Use Case Scandinavian Journalism Newsrooms combined with Seeding Generational Familiarity through Purpose-Driven Use in Education. This submission proposes a dual-entry approach to introduce the Meta-Layer and the Overweb to broad audiences by focusing on two interrelated use cases: education and journalism. First, in the school sector—particularly across Europe—project-based learning environments can integrate Meta-Layer tools to foster youth engagement with a safer, purpose-driven, and participatory web. Second, Scandinavia represents a particularly fertile region for a journalist-specific integration of the Meta-Layer. With strong traditions of collaborative civic culture, peaceful education systems, and relatively depolarized journalism ecosystems, countries like Denmark and Sweden could pioneer the use of a dedicated journalistic layer. This would empower newsrooms to verify content, trace sources, and resist the creep of misinformation. These use cases are mutually reinforcing and could provide a resilient first foothold for broader adoption. This submission was generated with protocol META-DP-EVAL-v1.3.

## Clarifications and Extensions

### DP10 – Education — Extension

**Integrative Use in Formal Curricula**

Proposes the Meta-Layer as a core educational tool embedded in curricula across sustainability, citizenship, and intercultural themes, enabling youth to use Meta-Layer tools for inquiry, annotation, and presentation.

*Why it matters:* Embedding the Meta-Layer in student learning ensures early familiarity with trustworthy digital practices and collaborative, ethical knowledge creation.

### DP14 – Trust and Transparency — Extension

**Scandinavian Journalism as a Pilot Region**

Suggests piloting the Meta-Layer with Scandinavian newsrooms due to civic trust and low polarization, allowing verification tools to enhance newsroom practices and public trust.

*Why it matters:* A low-risk, high-trust context offers an ideal environment for demonstrating the Meta-Layer's power in journalism, with models ready to be replicated globally.
