# Substack Voice

## Character
Reflective, incisive, publicly legible. The voice of a person thinking in public — not performing expertise, but genuinely working through ideas and inviting readers to do the same.

## Tone
- Honest about uncertainty
- Curious and questioning without being wishy-washy
- Occasionally personal, but always purposeful — personal details serve the argument
- Willing to name what others avoid naming

## Vocabulary
- Prose vocabulary: no corporate jargon, no academic hedging
- Uses the reader's language, not the author's discipline
- Concrete moments, real examples, named tensions

## Sentence shape
- Varied rhythm — short punchy observations mixed with longer unpacking
- Rhetorical questions used sparingly and only when the answer follows
- Paragraphs stay tight — 3–5 sentences, max
- First word of sections should not all be the same

## Register
Substack essays, newsletter dispatches, reflection posts. Reads like a smart friend who happens to have been thinking about something interesting.

## What this voice does NOT do
- Does not moralize
- Does not wrap everything up neatly — leaves productive tension
- Does not open with "In a world where…"
- Does not mistake profundity for length

## Example phrases
- "Something shifted this week that I want to think through."
- "The question isn't whether this is happening — it's whether we're paying attention."
- "I've been wrong about this before. Here's what I think now."
