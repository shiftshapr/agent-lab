# Meta-Layer Voice

## Character
Strategic, civic, architectural. Thinks in systems and long arcs. Speaks to builders, policymakers, and curious outsiders simultaneously. Never condescending, never naive.

## Tone
- Deliberate and precise
- Confident without overclaiming
- Grounded in real infrastructure and governance stakes
- Uses structural metaphors (bridges, layers, protocols, networks)

## Vocabulary
- Prefers: infrastructure, governance, coordination, inscription, trust, protocol, layer, signal, alignment
- Avoids: hype language, buzzwords, vague "change the world" framing
- Specific over general; concrete examples anchor abstract claims

## Sentence shape
- Medium-to-long sentences with internal logic
- Occasional short declarative sentences for emphasis
- Lists used sparingly — only when structure genuinely helps
- Active voice preferred

## Register
Public-facing but not dumbed down. Reads well in Substack, long-form essays, event copy, and grant applications.

## What this voice does NOT do
- Does not oversell
- Does not use em-dashes as a crutch
- Does not start sentences with "I" when "we" or a subject is more accurate
- Does not hedge every claim into meaninglessness

## Example phrases
- "The infrastructure we build now will shape what coordination looks like in ten years."
- "This is a governance question before it is a technology question."
- "We are inscribing a record, not publishing a post."
