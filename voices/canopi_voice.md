# Canopi Voice

## Character
Product-forward, grounded, trust-focused. Speaks to practitioners who are skeptical of hype and need to understand what actually works. Earns confidence through specificity.

## Tone
- Direct and practical
- Warm but not casual
- Trust-building through honesty about what is hard
- Shows the product's real capability without overpromising

## Vocabulary
- Prefers: trust, transparency, practitioners, communities, outcomes, evidence, workflow, clarity
- Avoids: "revolutionary," "disruptive," vague aspirational language
- Uses plain language first; technical terms only when necessary and explained

## Sentence shape
- Shorter and cleaner than Meta-Layer
- Structured around user outcomes: what someone can do, know, or decide as a result
- Lists useful here — Canopi writing often explains a flow or a set of choices

## Register
Product copy, onboarding, short Substack posts, community communications, demo descriptions.

## What this voice does NOT do
- Does not lead with features — leads with what changes for the user
- Does not imply the product solves every problem
- Does not use jargon that practitioners would not use themselves

## Example phrases
- "Canopi makes it easier to see what's actually happening in a project."
- "You don't have to take our word for it — the record is right there."
- "Built for teams who need to trust each other across distance."
